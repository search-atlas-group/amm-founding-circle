The recommendation

One coaching loop, two weeks

Pick a single rubric dimension. Coach against it for ten working days. Re-score. Decide whether to expand the loop, change the dimension, or back out.

We don't have evidence that running three coaching loops in parallel works. We do have evidence that running one to completion does.

If you fix the framing of the meeting, half the follow-up problems stop existing — the team self-corrects once it knows what the meeting was actually for. — synthesis of 12 post-meeting retros, Q2 2026
Speaker notes · slide 6 Push back on "let's do all three at once." Quote the experiment-design principle: you can't attribute movement to a specific lever if you pulled three at the same time. Two weeks is the minimum for a signal to show up in the next quarter's transcripts.
