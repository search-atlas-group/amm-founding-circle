# The Agentic Ladder (L1–10)

The one ladder for the cohort: **10 rungs, 4 tiers**. You self-place via the survey;
pods form by 2-rung band. Every skill in this repo is mapped to a rung — the repo is
the toolkit for the climb, the Knowledge Hub is the map.

Read the ladder money-first: each tier is a bigger revenue lever on the same
headcount, not a competency exam. The safety pieces at every rung — gates,
reviewers, rails, monitoring — are the seatbelt that lets you drive faster, never
the destination.

## The four tiers (and the one-word version)

If you want the whole ladder in plain words: you **Operate** the agent, it starts to
**Assist** on real client work, you **Orchestrate** several at once, the work
**Automates** itself against your spec, and eventually you **Command** a fleet from
your phone. Five words, four tiers — Foundation covers the first two flips at once.

| Tier | Rungs | What it means for your agency | One-word gloss |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | L1–L3 | Leave the chat window. Real client work, written rules, fully agentic in your own tools. | **Operate → Assist** |
| **Scale** | L4–L5 | Parallel agents with a check on the way out, then an always-on system that runs while you sleep. You stop being the bottleneck. | **Orchestrate** |
| **System** | L6–L8 | You write what "done" means; the system builds to it, checks itself, and remembers across runs. You review outcomes, not line-by-line. | **Automate** |
| **Autonomy** | L9–L10 | Multi-day unattended execution on rails you set — directed from your phone. You direct, you don't operate. | **Command** |

## The 10 rungs

- **Foundation — Operate → Assist**
  - **L1** — Real work, by voice, in version control
  - **L2** — The agent in your terminal (written rules, plans before it acts)
  - **L3** — Fully agentic (MCP + skills + tuned permissions; you've left the chat window)
- **Scale — Orchestrate**
  - **L4** — Orchestration (parallel agents with a gate)
  - **L5** — Always-on (hooks, monitoring, a morning briefing, hosted)
- **System — Automate**
  - **L6** — Spec-driven & self-checking
  - **L7** — Multi-model council
  - **L8** — End-to-end & remembering
- **Autonomy — Command**
  - **L9** — Multi-day, unattended, on-rails
  - **L10** — Commander-level orchestration from your phone

## How to climb with this repo

Every skill in this repo carries a suggested rung in the [skills index](../skills/README.md).
Treat it as a climb: run skills at your current band, then reach one rung up.
Single-purpose skills sit around L3–L4, orchestrators (`aeo-llm-content-planner`,
`client-onboarding-os`) around L6, and the autonomy/instinct skills higher.

A good week on the ladder looks like: ship one real client job with a skill at your
rung, then implement one workflow from the rung above — your way, not anyone else's
exact setup. Bring what broke to the next session; that's the curriculum.
